When we return to the UK in August 2027, we will have several revenue streams to tide us through until October 2030 (or a little earlier, for Jo) when we can start taking our actuarily reduced teachers' pensions. 

Our income streams will be £12k per year from our Airbnb and approximately £24k a year from bed-and-breakfasted ISAs, giving us a total annual income of £36k, none of this income will be taxed because it falls below our tax-free allowances or is withdrawn from our ISAs. This level of take-home pay is equivalent to a gross income of £45,630 (or roughly £22,815 each). We will, of course, still be paying off our mortgage, and as this costs £1,358.77 a month, our monthly combined passive income will drop to £1,641.23 (£3k - £1,358.77). 

£1,641.23 a month might be a little tight, so we plan to work part-time to make up the shortfall. Our monthly outgoings are £2,443.76 without the mortgage, so we will need to earn an extra £802.53 between us each month to keep the wolf from the door. 

By the time October 2030 comes around and we can start withdrawing our pensions, our income will increase by £1500 a month. We also plan at this point to pay off the mortgage, and so the financial outlook should be looking much rosier.

To model what will happen to our savings over the three bridge years:

We start with £200k in our General Investment Account (anything above £200k having been used to purchase vehicles)

We immediately put £40k of this into our respective ISAs

We withdraw £1k a month each from our respective ISAs, adding £2k/month to our income

At the end of the year, our GIA stands at £160k (£200k - £40k) + any growth 

Our ISAs stand at £8k each, with both £20k pots having been subjected to 12 monthly withdrawals of £1k.

This process repeats for the following 2 years, so that the figures become:

### End of Year 2 (August 2029):
* **GIA:** Stands at £120k (£160k - £40k) + any growth.
* **ISAs:** Stand at £16k each (the remaining £8k from Year 1 + a new £20k injection, minus 12 monthly withdrawals of £1k).

### End of Year 3 (August 2030):
* **GIA:** Stands at £80k (£120k - £40k) + any growth.
* **ISAs:** Stand at £24k each (the remaining £16k from Year 2 + a final £20k injection, minus 12 monthly withdrawals of £1k).

At the end of Year 3, the total funds remaining across our ISAs and GIA accounts **£128,000** (plus whatever investment growth has accumulated over those three years).

Here is how that splits down:
* **GIA:** £80,000
* **ISAs (Combined):** £48,000 (£24,000 each)
* **Total:** **£128,000**

Our Mortgage at this stage will be £141,720, allowing us to pay the whole thing off with the combined contents of our ISAs, GIA and TPS lump sum, leaving **£36,280** entirely clear in our savings (plus whatever investment growth has accumulated over those three years), with the house completely unencumbered and our monthly outgoings permanently slashed by £1,358.77.

So the picture post August 2030 will look like this:

* **Housing:** Our home will be completely mortgage-free. The **£1,358.77** monthly payment is entirely gone.
* **Liquid Capital:** Our GIA and ISAs are reset to zero, having done their job to clear the debt. We are left with a clean, unencumbered cash buffer of **£36,280** (the remainder of the TPS lump sum plus three years of investment growth from the bridge accounts) sitting ready to be re-invested or used as you see fit.
* **SIPP Boost:** Our pensions will have successfully received an extra **£36,000** total (£18,000 each) over the three-year gap, funded straight out of Jeeves Property Management.
* **Monthly Outgoings:** Essential living costs hover at around **£2,443.76**.
* **Guaranteed Passive Income:** Our monthly income is driven by property and pension, without touching capital:
    * **Airbnb (Llangollen):** £1,000 pcm
    * **BTL (Bangor via Jeeves):** £1,000 pcm
    * **Teachers' Pension Scheme:** £1,500 pcm
    * **Total Income:** **£3,500 per month** (£42,000 a year).
